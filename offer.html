If box dye, the salon, or another gray shampoo let you down, it was not your fault.

Regular dye colors your hair by bleaching it. Peroxide and ammonia crack the strand open and burn through the protein inside, so it goes hollow, like a straw. That is why dyed hair turns dry and snaps. And the ingredient that actually makes the color, PPD, is the number one cause of hair-dye allergy.

Other gray shampoos just coat the surface, so they rinse out in a week.

Lumière does the opposite. It lays plant pigment on top to cover the gray, then pushes keratin and collagen inside to pack the strand back out. Color on the outside, repair on the inside, in one wash.

Questions women ask before they start

Will it cover my stubborn, wiry grays?
Yes, though give it time. Because coverage builds a little with every wash, even the resistant grays around your part and temples fill in gradually, instead of being blasted with peroxide to force it. Most women reach full coverage in 2 to 3 weeks.
Is it really PPD-free and safe for a sensitive scalp?
Yes. Lumière is made without PPD, resorcinol, peroxide and ammonia, the ingredients behind most hair-dye irritation and reactions. It is designed for sensitive scalps. As with any new hair product, we still recommend a simple patch test first.
Will it turn my hair orange or brassy?
No. Brass comes from oxidative dyes that lift and bleach the strand. Lumière does not lift or bleach, it lays down true-to-tone plant pigment, so the color reads natural, no orange and no helmet.
How long does the coverage last?
It builds and holds with regular use, and it grows out softly with no hard line, so there is no root-touch-up panic. Keep it up with 2 to 3 washes a week.
How is this different from box dye?
Box dye uses peroxide and PPD to force color in, which damages the strand and can irritate your scalp. Lumière deposits plant pigment on top and rebuilds the strand from the inside, so you cover the gray and repair the hair at the same time.
How do I use it, and how often?
Use it in the shower like a shampoo. Massage in, leave 3 to 5 minutes, and rinse. Use it every wash while your color builds, then 2 to 3 times a week to maintain.
